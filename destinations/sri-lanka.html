Sri Lanka, known as the “Pearl of the Indian Ocean,” is a destination that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From the ancient ruins of Sigiriya to the pristine beaches of Bentota, Sri Lanka is a land of diverse experiences that captivate every traveller. Our Sri Lanka tour packages from India are meticulously crafted to provide you with an immersive journey, ensuring that you experience the very best of this tropical paradise.

Explore Sri Lanka's Rich Cultural Heritage

Sri Lanka is home to a wealth of cultural treasures, and our tour packages offer the opportunity to explore the country’s most iconic historical sites. Begin your journey in the Cultural Triangle, where you can visit the ancient rock fortress of Sigiriya, a UNESCO World Heritage site that rises dramatically from the surrounding plains. Climb to the top for panoramic views and explore the well-preserved frescoes and gardens.

Visit the sacred city of Kandy, where you can explore the Temple of the Tooth Relic, one of the most important Buddhist sites in Sri Lanka. The city is also known for its beautiful botanical gardens, vibrant markets, and traditional cultural performances.

Relax on Sri Lanka's Stunning Beaches

Sri Lanka is blessed with some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, and our tour packages include visits to the country’s top coastal destinations. Relax on the golden sands of Bentota, known for its calm waters and luxury resorts. For a more laid-back vibe, visit Mirissa, where you can enjoy whale watching, snorkelling, and sunset cruises.

The southern coast of Sri Lanka is home to Galle, a historic colonial town with a charming old-world atmosphere. Explore the well-preserved Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and stroll through the narrow streets lined with cafes, boutiques, and art galleries.

Experience Sri Lanka's Diverse Wildlife

Sri Lanka is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts, with a variety of national parks and reserves that are home to diverse flora and fauna. Our tour packages include safaris in Yala National Park, where you can spot elephants, leopards, and a wide range of bird species. Visit Udawalawe National Park, known for its large elephant population, or explore the wetlands of Bundala National Park, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.

For a unique experience, visit the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage, where you can observe elephants being cared for and even participate in feeding them.

Must-Visit Destinations in Sri Lanka

Sigiriya

Begin your journey with a visit to Sigiriya, one of Sri Lanka’s most iconic landmarks. This ancient rock fortress, also known as the “Lion Rock,” rises 200 metres above the surrounding plains and offers stunning views of the landscape. Climb to the top to explore the ruins of King Kasyapa’s palace, admire the well-preserved frescoes, and stroll through the terraced gardens.

Kandy

Kandy, the cultural capital of Sri Lanka, is home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic, one of the most important Buddhist sites in the country. Explore the temple complex, visit the Royal Botanical Gardens in Peradeniya, and enjoy a traditional Kandyan dance performance. Kandy is also a great base for exploring the nearby Knuckles Mountain Range, a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its biodiversity and scenic hiking trails.

Galle

The historic city of Galle is a must-visit destination on Sri Lanka’s southern coast. Explore the well-preserved Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage site that dates back to the 16th century. Wander through the narrow streets of the old town, where you’ll find charming cafes, art galleries, and boutique shops. The beaches around Galle are also ideal for relaxation, with Unawatuna and Thalpe being popular choices.

Nuwara Eliya

Known as “Little England,” Nuwara Eliya is a hill station in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. The town is famous for its colonial-era architecture, cool climate, and lush tea plantations. Take a scenic train ride through the tea country, visit a working tea factory, and enjoy a leisurely boat ride on Gregory Lake. Nuwara Eliya is also the gateway to Horton Plains National Park, where you can hike to World’s End, a dramatic cliff with breathtaking views.

Practical Travel Tips for Sri Lanka

Best Time to Visit: Sri Lanka’s climate is influenced by two monsoon seasons, so the best time to visit depends on the region you plan to explore. The west and south coasts, as well as the central highlands, are best visited from December to March. The east coast is ideal from April to September. However, Sri Lanka is a year-round destination, and different regions offer good weather at different times of the year.

Visa Requirements: Indian citizens travelling to Sri Lanka require an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA), which allows for a stay of up to 30 days. The application process is online and straightforward, but it’s recommended to apply for the ETA well in advance of your travel dates.

Language and Communication: Sinhala and Tamil are the official languages of Sri Lanka, but English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. Learning a few basic Sinhala or Tamil phrases can enhance your experience and help you connect with locals.

Currency: The currency in Sri Lanka is the Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). Credit and debit cards are widely accepted in major cities, but it’s advisable to carry some cash for small purchases, especially in rural areas and markets.

Transportation: Sri Lanka has a well-connected transportation network, including buses, trains, and domestic flights. Trains are a scenic way to travel between destinations, especially on routes like Colombo to Kandy and Kandy to Ella. Tuk-tuks are a popular and convenient option for short distances.

Cultural Etiquette: Sri Lankan culture values politeness and respect. Always remove your shoes before entering temples and other sacred sites. It’s customary to greet people with a smile and to use your right hand for giving and receiving items.

Safety: Sri Lanka is generally a safe destination for travellers, but it’s essential to stay cautious and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Culinary Delights: Sri Lankan cuisine is rich and diverse, with influences from Indian, Arab, and European cooking. Don’t miss trying traditional dishes like rice and curry, hoppers, and kottu roti.

Festivals and Events: Sri Lanka hosts various festivals throughout the year, including the Sinhala and Tamil New Year in April, the Kandy Esala Perahera in August, and the Colombo International Book Fair in September.

Accommodation: Sri Lanka offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts to budget guesthouses and boutique hotels. Booking your stay in advance is advisable, especially during peak tourist seasons.
