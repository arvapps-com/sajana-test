Discover the Best of Georgia: Exclusive Tour Packages from India

Nestled at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, Georgia is a land of breathtaking landscapes, ancient history, and vibrant culture. Known for its stunning mountain ranges, charming towns, and world-renowned wine, Georgia is a destination that offers something for every traveller. Our Georgia tour packages from India are crafted to provide you with an unforgettable experience, combining the best of Georgian hospitality, scenic beauty, and cultural richness.

Experience the Charm of Tbilisi

Your journey to Georgia begins in Tbilisi, the capital city known for its unique blend of ancient and modern architecture. With our expertly designed Georgia tour packages, you can explore the narrow, cobblestone streets of the Old Town, where historic churches, sulphur baths, and traditional houses stand alongside trendy cafes and boutiques. Visit the iconic Narikala Fortress for panoramic views of the city, and stroll along Rustaveli Avenue, the cultural heart of Tbilisi.

For those interested in history and culture, Tbilisi offers a wealth of museums and galleries. The Georgian National Museum houses an impressive collection of artefacts that tell the story of Georgia's rich past, while the Open Air Museum of Ethnography provides a glimpse into the traditional lifestyles of Georgian communities.

Venture into Georgia's Stunning Landscapes

Georgia's diverse landscapes are a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Our Georgia tour packages from India include visits to some of the country's most picturesque regions. Explore the lush vineyards of Kakheti, Georgia's premier wine-producing region, where you can taste some of the world's finest wines and learn about the ancient tradition of winemaking.

For those seeking adventure, the Caucasus Mountains offer a range of outdoor activities. Hike through the scenic trails of Kazbegi National Park, where you'll be rewarded with stunning views of Mount Kazbek, or visit the remote village of Ushguli in Svaneti, one of the highest inhabited places in Europe, famous for its mediaeval watchtowers and breathtaking landscapes.

Immerse Yourself in Georgian Culture

Georgia is a country with a deep-rooted culture that is reflected in its music, dance, and cuisine. Our Georgia tour packages offer opportunities to experience this rich cultural heritage firsthand. Attend a traditional Georgian supra (feast), where you can enjoy local dishes like khachapuri (cheese-filled bread) and khinkali (dumplings), accompanied by toasts of wine led by a tamada (toastmaster).

Music and dance are integral parts of Georgian culture, and no trip to Georgia is complete without witnessing a performance of traditional Georgian polyphonic singing or the dynamic movements of Georgian dance. These cultural experiences are not just entertainment—they are a window into the soul of Georgia.

Must-Visit Destinations in Georgia

Tbilisi

Start your journey in Tbilisi, Georgia’s vibrant capital. The city is a melting pot of cultures, where East meets West in a captivating blend of history and modernity. Key attractions include the Old Town, where you can explore narrow alleyways lined with colourful houses and traditional balconies. Don't miss the Tbilisi Sulphur Baths, a centuries-old tradition that offers relaxation in naturally heated waters.

Kazbegi (Stepantsminda)

Located in the heart of the Caucasus Mountains, Kazbegi is a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers. Visit the Gergeti Trinity Church, perched high on a hill with the majestic Mount Kazbek as a backdrop. The area offers excellent hiking opportunities, with trails that take you through breathtaking landscapes.

Mtskheta

Just a short drive from Tbilisi, Mtskheta is one of the oldest cities in Georgia and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is home to some of Georgia's most important religious landmarks, including the Svetitskhoveli Cathedral and the Jvari Monastery, both of which are significant examples of early Christian architecture.

Batumi

For a different side of Georgia, head to Batumi, a lively seaside city on the Black Sea coast. Known for its modern architecture, botanical gardens, and vibrant nightlife, Batumi offers a refreshing contrast to Georgia's historical inland cities. Stroll along the Batumi Boulevard, visit the Alphabet Tower, and enjoy the city's bustling atmosphere.

Svaneti

This remote region in northwestern Georgia is known for its stunning natural beauty and well-preserved mediaeval villages. The Upper Svaneti region, with its unique tower houses and dramatic landscapes, is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Explore the village of Mestia and take a trek to the Chaladi Glacier for an unforgettable adventure.

Practical Travel Tips for Georgia

Best Time to Visit: Georgia is a year-round destination, but the best times to visit are in the spring (April to June) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are lush and vibrant. Summer is ideal for trekking and outdoor activities, while winter offers skiing opportunities in the Caucasus Mountains.

Visa Requirements: Indian citizens can obtain an e-visa to travel to Georgia, which is valid for stays of up to 30 days. The e-visa process is simple and can be completed online before your trip.

Language and Communication: The official language of Georgia is Georgian, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas, especially in Tbilisi and Batumi. Learning a few basic Georgian phrases can enhance your experience and help you connect with locals.

Currency: The official currency of Georgia is the Georgian Lari (GEL). Credit cards are accepted in major cities, but it's advisable to carry cash when travelling to rural areas.

Transportation: Georgia has an extensive network of buses, minibosses (marshrutkas), and trains that connect major cities and towns. Taxis are also widely available and relatively inexpensive. For more flexibility, consider renting a car to explore the country at your own pace.

Cultural Etiquette: Georgians are known for their warm hospitality. When visiting someone’s home, it's customary to bring a small gift, such as flowers or sweets. When visiting churches and monasteries, dress modestly and respect the local customs.

Safety: Georgia is generally a safe country for travellers, with low crime rates. However, it's always wise to take standard precautions, such as keeping your belongings secure and being aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded places.

Culinary Delights: Georgian cuisine is a highlight of any trip to the country. Must-try dishes include khachapuri (cheese bread), khinkali (dumplings), and churchkhela (a traditional candy made of nuts and grape juice).
