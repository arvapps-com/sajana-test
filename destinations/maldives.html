Must-Visit Destinations in Maldives

Malé City

Begin your journey in Malé, the capital city of Maldives. Known for its vibrant culture and rich history, Malé offers a blend of modernity and tradition. Explore the local markets, visit the Maldives Islamic Centre, and admire the stunning architecture of the Grand Friday Mosque. Don't miss the opportunity to experience the bustling atmosphere of the city and enjoy some delicious Maldivian cuisine.

Baa Atoll

Baa Atoll is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, famous for its incredible biodiversity and marine life. This destination is ideal for snorkelling and diving, where you can encounter manta rays, whale sharks, and vibrant coral reefs. Experience the tranquil beauty of this atoll, known for its pristine beaches and luxurious resorts, making it a perfect getaway for relaxation and adventure.

Banana Reef

Banana Reef is one of the most popular dive sites in the Maldives, known for its stunning underwater scenery and diverse marine life. Here, divers can explore vibrant coral gardens and encounter various fish species, including parrotfish, barracuda, and even the occasional reef shark. This site is perfect for both beginners and experienced divers looking for an unforgettable underwater experience.

Sunset Dolphin Cruise

Experience the magic of the Maldives on a sunset dolphin cruise. As the sun sets over the horizon, watch playful dolphins dance alongside your boat. This unforgettable experience allows you to enjoy the breathtaking beauty of the Maldivian waters while spotting these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.

Practical Travel Tips for Maldives

Best Time to Visit: The Maldives is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the dry season from November to April when the weather is pleasant and perfect for water activities.

Visa Requirements: Indian citizens can obtain a tourist visa on arrival for 30 days. Make sure to have a valid passport, accommodation details, and a return ticket.

Language and Communication: The official language is Dhivehi, but English is widely spoken, especially in resorts and tourist areas. Learning a few basic phrases can enhance your experience.

Currency: The currency in the Maldives is the Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR). However, US dollars are widely accepted in resorts and shops.

Transportation: The Maldives consists of many islands, and transportation between them is primarily by speedboat or seaplane. Plan your transfers in advance to ensure smooth travel.

Cultural Etiquette: Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting local islands. Dress modestly and always ask for permission before taking photos of people.

Safety: The Maldives is generally safe for travellers, but it's essential to stay vigilant and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Culinary Delights: Maldivian cuisine is a blend of flavors, influenced by Indian and Sri Lankan dishes. Don't miss trying traditional dishes like Garudhiya (fish soup), Mas Huni (tuna salad), and fresh seafood.

Festivals and Events: The Maldives hosts various festivals throughout the year, including Ramadan, Eid celebrations, and the Maldives National Day on 26th July.

Accommodation: The Maldives offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts to guesthouses on local islands. It's advisable to book your stay in advance, especially during peak season.
