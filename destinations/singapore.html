Experience the Best of Singapore: Tailored Tour Packages from India

Singapore, a dazzling city-state known for its futuristic skyline, vibrant culture, and lush green spaces, offers a perfect blend of modernity and tradition. From world-class shopping and dining to iconic landmarks and serene gardens, Singapore has something for everyone. Our Singapore tour packages from India are expertly crafted to provide you with an unforgettable journey, ensuring that you experience the very best of this dynamic destination.

Explore Iconic Landmarks

Your Singapore adventure begins with a visit to some of the city's most iconic landmarks. Marvel at the architectural wonder of Marina Bay Sands, with its rooftop infinity pool offering breathtaking views of the city. Explore Gardens by the Bay, a stunning nature park that features the futuristic Supertree Grove, Cloud Forest, and Flower Dome. Don't miss the light and sound show at the Supertree Grove, a mesmerising experience that illuminates the night sky.

Visit Sentosa Island, Singapore's premier resort destination, where you can relax on pristine beaches, visit the S.E.A. Aquarium, or experience the thrills of Universal Studios Singapore. The island is also home to the Merlion, Singapore's iconic symbol, where you can take in panoramic views of the city from the Merlion Tower.

Immerse Yourself in Cultural Diversity

Singapore's cultural diversity is one of its greatest strengths, and our tour packages provide opportunities to explore the city's rich heritage. Discover the vibrant neighbourhoods of Chinatown, Little India, and Kampong Glam, each offering a unique glimpse into the cultural fabric of Singapore.

In Chinatown, visit the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple and Museum, explore the bustling street markets, and savour authentic Chinese cuisine. In Little India, immerse yourself in the colourful streets adorned with intricate temples and vibrant murals, and indulge in delicious South Indian dishes. Kampong Glam, the historic Malay-Arab quarter, is home to the stunning Sultan Mosque and trendy Haji Lane, known for its eclectic boutiques and cafes.

Experience World-Class Shopping and Dining

Singapore is a shopper's paradise, offering everything from luxury brands to unique local finds. Orchard Road, Singapore's premier shopping street, is lined with high-end boutiques, department stores, and shopping malls. For a more eclectic shopping experience, visit Bugis Street Market or Haji Lane, where you can find trendy fashion, accessories, and souvenirs.

Food is at the heart of Singaporean culture, and our tour packages include opportunities to savour the city's diverse culinary offerings. From hawker centres serving mouthwatering local dishes like Hainanese chicken rice and laksa to Michelin-starred restaurants offering gourmet dining experiences, Singapore is a food lover's dream. Don't forget to try the iconic Singapore Sling at the historic Raffles Hotel.

Your Complete Singapore Travel Guide: Top Destinations and Essential Tips

Marina Bay Sands and Gardens by the Bay

Begin your journey at Marina Bay Sands, one of Singapore's most iconic landmarks. Take in panoramic views of the city from the SkyPark Observation Deck, relax by the rooftop infinity pool, or indulge in world-class dining and shopping. Just a short walk away, Gardens by the Bay offers a green oasis in the heart of the city, with its towering Supertrees, exotic plant species, and the spectacular Cloud Forest and Flower Dome conservatories.

Sentosa Island

Sentosa Island is a must-visit for families and thrill-seekers alike. Explore Universal Studios Singapore, where you can enjoy themed rides and attractions, or visit Adventure Cove Waterpark for a day of aquatic fun. Sentosa is also home to the S.E.A. Aquarium, one of the largest aquariums in the world, where you can discover marine life from the depths of the ocean.

Chinatown, Little India, and Kampong Glam

Singapore's multicultural heritage is best experienced in its vibrant ethnic neighbourhoods. In Chinatown, explore the rich history and culture at the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple, shop for souvenirs at the street markets, and sample traditional Chinese delicacies. Little India offers a sensory feast with its colourful streets, fragrant spices, and ornate temples. Kampong Glam, the historic Malay-Arab quarter, is a trendy area with the majestic Sultan Mosque, boutique shops, and a lively cafe scene.

Practical Travel Tips for Singapore

Best Time to Visit: Singapore's tropical climate means it is warm and humid year-round, with occasional rain showers. The best time to visit is from February to April when the weather is relatively dry and comfortable. However, Singapore is a year-round destination, and most attractions are accessible regardless of the season.

Visa Requirements: Indian citizens travelling to Singapore require a visa. The application process is straightforward and can be completed online or through an authorised travel agency. It is advisable to apply for the visa well in advance of your travel dates.

Language and Communication: Singapore has four official languages: English, Mandarin, Malay, and Tamil. English is widely spoken and used for official communication, making it easy for Indian travellers to navigate the city.

Currency: The currency in Singapore is the Singapore Dollar (SGD). Credit and debit cards are widely accepted, and ATMs are readily available throughout the city. It is advisable to carry some cash for small purchases and transportation.

Transportation: Singapore has an efficient and reliable public transportation system, including buses, trains (MRT), and taxis. The MRT is the fastest and most convenient way to get around the city. For added convenience, consider purchasing an EZ-Link card, which can be used on all public transport.

Cultural Etiquette: Singapore is a multicultural society with a mix of different cultures and religions. It is important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites. Dress modestly in places of worship, remove your shoes before entering homes and temples, and avoid public displays of affection.

Safety: Singapore is one of the safest cities in the world, with low crime rates and a clean environment. However, it is still important to take standard precautions, such as keeping your belongings secure and being aware of your surroundings.
