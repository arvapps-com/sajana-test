Experience the Magic of Finland: Exclusive Tour Packages from India

Finland, the land of a thousand lakes, is a destination that captivates with its natural beauty, unique culture, and enchanting winter wonderland. From the mesmerising Northern Lights to the pristine wilderness of Lapland, Finland offers experiences that are both awe-inspiring and unforgettable. Our Finland tour packages from India are meticulously crafted to provide you with an immersive adventure, ensuring that you experience the best of what this Nordic gem has to offer.

Witness the Northern Lights

One of the most magical experiences Finland offers is witnessing the Aurora Borealis, or Northern Lights. Our Finland tour packages include opportunities to see this natural phenomenon in all its glory. Whether you're staying in a glass igloo in Saariselkä or a cosy log cabin in Rovaniemi, the Northern Lights are an unforgettable sight that will leave you in awe. The best time to see the Northern Lights in Finland is from September to March, when the nights are longest and the skies are clearest.

Explore the Winter Wonderland of Lapland

Lapland, Finland's northernmost region, is a winter paradise that looks like it was plucked straight out of a fairy tale. Our tour packages offer a range of activities that allow you to fully embrace the winter magic of Lapland. Ride a husky sled through snow-covered forests, visit a reindeer farm and learn about Sami culture, or take a snowmobile safari across frozen lakes. For a truly unique experience, spend a night in an ice hotel, where everything from the beds to the glasses at the bar are made of ice.

For families, no visit to Lapland is complete without a trip to Santa Claus Village in Rovaniemi, where you can meet Santa himself, send postcards from the official Santa Claus Post Office, and cross the Arctic Circle.

Discover Finland's Pristine Nature

Beyond the winter attractions, Finland is a country of stunning natural beauty that can be enjoyed year-round. Our Finland tour packages from India include visits to some of the country's most beautiful national parks, such as Nuuksio National Park and Koli National Park. Here, you can hike through ancient forests, kayak on crystal-clear lakes, and enjoy the tranquillity of the Finnish wilderness.

In the summer, experience the midnight sun in Lapland, where the sun never sets for several weeks, creating a unique atmosphere perfect for outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and berry picking. Finland's archipelago, one of the largest in the world, is another must-visit during the summer, offering endless opportunities for island hopping, sailing, and exploring charming coastal villages.

Immerse Yourself in Finnish Culture

Finnish culture is deeply connected to nature and tradition. Our tour packages provide opportunities to experience this culture firsthand. Visit Helsinki, Finland's capital, known for its stunning architecture, vibrant design scene, and rich history. Explore landmarks such as the Helsinki Cathedral, Suomenlinna Sea Fortress, and the contemporary art museum, Kiasma.

No trip to Finland is complete without experiencing the Finnish sauna, a quintessential part of Finnish life. Relax in a lakeside sauna and then take a refreshing dip in the cool waters, just as the Finns do.

Your Complete Finland Travel Guide: Top Destinations and Essential Tips

Finland, a country where the natural beauty of the north meets modern design and culture, is an ideal destination for travellers seeking both adventure and tranquillity. Whether you're planning to explore the wintry landscapes of Lapland, experience the vibrant cities, or relax in the serene Finnish countryside, our Finland travel guide provides all the information you need to make the most of your trip. From must-see destinations to practical travel advice, this guide will ensure your Finnish adventure is both well-planned and unforgettable.

Must-Visit Destinations in Finland

Helsinki

Start your journey in Helsinki, the capital of Finland. Known for its neoclassical architecture, vibrant arts scene, and innovative design, Helsinki is a city that blends the old and new seamlessly. Explore the Helsinki Cathedral, take a ferry to the Suomenlinna Sea Fortress, and visit the Design District, where you can shop for unique Finnish designs and enjoy local cuisine.

Rovaniemi

Located in the heart of Lapland, Rovaniemi is known as the official hometown of Santa Claus. Here, you can visit Santa Claus Village, cross the Arctic Circle, and experience the magic of the Northern Lights. Rovaniemi also offers a range of winter activities, including husky sledding, reindeer safaris, and ice fishing.

Turku

As Finland’s oldest city, Turku is rich in history and culture. Visit the mediaeval Turku Castle, explore the vibrant riverside market square, and take a cruise through the beautiful Turku Archipelago. Turku is also a gateway to the Aland Islands, an autonomous region known for its unique culture and stunning landscapes.

Lapland

Beyond Rovaniemi, Lapland offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventure and natural beauty. Visit Levi, one of Finland's premier ski resorts, or head to the remote wilderness areas of Inari and Kilpisjärvi, where you can experience the midnight sun in summer or chase the Northern Lights in winter.

Lake Saimaa and the Finnish Lakeland

Known as the land of a thousand lakes, Finland’s Lakeland region is perfect for those seeking peace and tranquillity. Explore the stunning Lake Saimaa, Finland’s largest lake, where you can kayak, fish, and even spot the endangered Saimaa ringed seal. The region is also home to charming towns like Savonlinna, known for its mediaeval Olavinlinna Castle.

Practical Travel Tips for Finland

Best Time to Visit: Finland is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on the experiences you seek. Winter, from December to March, is perfect for witnessing the Northern Lights and enjoying winter sports in Lapland. Summer, from June to August, is ideal for experiencing the midnight sun, exploring national parks, and enjoying outdoor activities.

Visa Requirements: Indian citizens travelling to Finland require a Schengen visa, as Finland is part of the Schengen Area. The visa allows travel to Finland and other Schengen countries for up to 90 days. It's recommended to apply for the visa well in advance of your travel dates.

Language and Communication: Finnish and Swedish are the official languages of Finland, but English is widely spoken, especially in cities and tourist areas. Learning a few basic Finnish phrases can enhance your experience and help you connect with locals.

Currency: The currency in Finland is the Euro (EUR). Credit cards are widely accepted, and ATMs are readily available. It's advisable to carry some cash when travelling to rural areas.

Transportation: Finland has an efficient public transportation system, including trains, buses, and domestic flights that connect major cities and regions. For exploring rural areas and national parks, renting a car provides greater flexibility.

Cultural Etiquette: Finns are known for their politeness, punctuality, and respect for personal space. When visiting a Finnish home, it's customary to remove your shoes at the door. Saunas are an integral part of Finnish culture, and it’s common to take a sauna with friends or family.

Safety: Finland is one of the safest countries in the world for travellers, with low crime rates. However, it's important to take standard precautions, such as securing your belongings and following safety guidelines for outdoor activities, especially in winter conditions.

Culinary Delights: Finnish cuisine is a delightful blend of traditional and modern flavours. Must-try dishes include karjalanpiirakka (Karelian pasties), lohikeitto (salmon soup), and various berry desserts.

Festivals and Events: Finland hosts a variety of festivals and events throughout the year, including Juhannus (Midsummer) in June, the Helsinki Festival in August, and the Rovaniemi Christmas Market in December.

Accommodation: Finland offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to cozy cabins and charming guesthouses. It’s advisable to book your stay in advance, especially during peak seasons.
