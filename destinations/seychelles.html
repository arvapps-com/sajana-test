Experience the Best of Seychelles: Exclusive Tour Packages from India

Seychelles, a tropical paradise nestled in the Indian Ocean, is the ultimate destination for travellers seeking sun-kissed beaches, crystal-clear waters, and unspoiled natural beauty. Whether you're planning a romantic getaway, a family vacation, or an adventure-filled escape, our Seychelles tour packages from India are meticulously crafted to provide you with a seamless and immersive journey, ensuring that you experience the best of this stunning archipelago.

Relax on the Pristine Beaches of Seychelles

Seychelles is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, and our tour packages offer the opportunity to unwind on the soft, white sands of these idyllic islands. Anse Lazio on Praslin Island, consistently ranked among the world's best beaches, is known for its clear waters, granite boulders, and vibrant marine life. Spend your days swimming, snorkelling, or simply soaking in the breathtaking scenery.

For those seeking a more secluded experience, La Digue Island offers the tranquil Anse Source d'Argent, a beach famous for its pink-hued sands and unique rock formations. The island’s laid-back atmosphere and stunning landscapes make it the perfect place to relax and reconnect with nature.

Explore Seychelles’ Lush Nature and Marine Life

Beyond its beaches, Seychelles is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Our Seychelles tour packages include visits to some of the archipelago’s most beautiful natural reserves. Discover the Vallée de Mai on Praslin Island, a UNESCO World Heritage site, where you can walk through a prehistoric forest and see the rare coco de mer palm, unique to Seychelles.

For those interested in marine life, a trip to the Sainte Anne Marine National Park offers an unforgettable experience. Snorkel or dive among vibrant coral reefs teeming with colourful fish, sea turtles, and other marine creatures. The park is also an excellent spot for glass-bottom boat tours, providing a window into the underwater world without getting wet.

Experience Seychelles’ Unique Culture and Cuisine

Seychelles is a melting pot of cultures, with influences from Africa, Europe, and Asia. Our tour packages provide opportunities to experience this rich cultural heritage firsthand. Visit the capital city, Victoria, on Mahé Island, where you can explore the bustling market, visit the Seychelles National Botanical Gardens, and admire the historic clock tower, a symbol of the island’s colonial past.

Seychelles’ cuisine is a delightful fusion of flavours, with fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and spices taking centre stage. Enjoy traditional Creole dishes like grilled fish, octopus curry, and coconut-based desserts at local restaurants or during a beachside barbecue.

Must-Visit Destinations in Seychelles

Mahé Island

Begin your journey on Mahé, the largest island in Seychelles and home to the capital, Victoria. Explore the Seychelles National Botanical Gardens, where you can see giant tortoises and rare orchids, and visit the Victoria Market to experience the local culture and cuisine. Mahé also boasts some of the best beaches in Seychelles, including Beau Vallon, a popular spot for swimming, snorkelling, and water sports.

Praslin Island

Praslin is known for its stunning beaches and lush nature. Visit the Vallée de Mai, a UNESCO World Heritage site, where you can walk through a prehistoric forest and see the coco de mer palm, unique to Seychelles. Anse Lazio, one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, is a must-visit for its crystal-clear waters and perfect swimming conditions.

La Digue Island

La Digue is a laid-back island known for its beautiful beaches and relaxed atmosphere. Anse Source d'Argent, with its pink sands and granite boulders, is one of the most photographed beaches in the world. Explore the island by bicycle, visit the historic L’Union Estate, and take a boat trip to the nearby island of Grande Soeur for a day of snorkelling and picnicking.

Practical Travel Tips for Seychelles

Best Time to Visit: Seychelles is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on what you want to experience. April, May, October, and November offer the best weather for beach activities, with calm seas and warm temperatures. June to September is ideal for sailing and water sports, while the period from November to March is great for diving, as the waters are clearer and visibility is at its best.

Visa Requirements: Indian citizens travelling to Seychelles do not require a visa for stays of up to 30 days. However, you must have a valid passport, return or onward ticket, and proof of accommodation and sufficient funds for your stay. A Visitor’s Permit will be issued on arrival at no cost.

Language and Communication: The official languages of Seychelles are English, French, and Seychellois Creole. English is widely spoken and used for official communication, making it easy for Indian travellers to navigate the islands.

Currency: The currency in Seychelles is the Seychellois Rupee (SCR). Credit and debit cards are widely accepted in hotels, restaurants, and shops, but it’s advisable to carry some cash for small purchases, especially on the smaller islands. ATMs are available in Victoria and major tourist areas.

Transportation: Seychelles has a well-developed transportation network, including domestic flights, ferries, and buses. Ferries are a popular way to travel between the islands of Mahé, Praslin, and La Digue. On the islands, bicycles, taxis, and rental cars are common modes of transport. Frequently Asked Questions

▶ What are the top attractions to visit in Seychelles?
