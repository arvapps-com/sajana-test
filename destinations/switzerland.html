Discover Switzerland’s Majestic Alps

Switzerland is synonymous with the Alps, and our tour packages offer the opportunity to explore some of the most iconic mountain landscapes in the world. Begin your journey in Zermatt, home to the Matterhorn, one of the most famous peaks in the Alps. Take the Gornergrat Railway to enjoy panoramic views of the Matterhorn and surrounding mountains, or venture onto the slopes for skiing, snowboarding, or hiking.

For a truly unique experience, visit Jungfraujoch, known as the “Top of Europe.” Located at an altitude of 3,454 metres, Jungfraujoch offers breathtaking views of the Aletsch Glacier, the longest glacier in the Alps. The journey to Jungfraujoch includes a scenic train ride through the Swiss countryside, with stops in the charming villages of Lauterbrunnen and Grindelwald.

Explore Switzerland’s Pristine Lakes and Charming Cities

Switzerland is home to some of the most beautiful lakes in Europe, and our tour packages include visits to the country’s top lakeside destinations. Lake Geneva, one of the largest lakes in Europe, is surrounded by vineyards, charming towns, and the cosmopolitan city of Geneva. Explore the mediaeval town of Montreux, visit the Château de Chillon, and take a boat cruise on the lake to enjoy the stunning scenery.

Zurich, Switzerland’s largest city, offers a perfect blend of urban sophistication and natural beauty. Stroll through the historic Old Town, visit world-class museums and galleries, and enjoy shopping along Bahnhofstrasse, one of the world’s most exclusive shopping streets. Zurich is also a great base for exploring nearby attractions, including Lake Zurich and the Rhine Falls, the largest waterfall in Europe.

Experience Switzerland’s Rich Culture and Cuisine

Switzerland’s culture is a unique blend of German, French, and Italian influences, and our tour packages provide opportunities to experience this rich cultural heritage firsthand. Visit Lucerne, a charming city known for its preserved mediaeval architecture, including the iconic Chapel Bridge and Water Tower. Explore the Swiss Transport Museum, take a boat cruise on Lake Lucerne, and ride the world’s steepest cogwheel railway to the summit of Mount Pilatus.

Swiss cuisine is renowned for its quality and variety, with dishes like fondue, raclette, and rösti taking centre stage. Enjoy a traditional Swiss meal in a cosy mountain restaurant, or sample fine Swiss chocolates and cheeses at local markets and specialty shops.

Must-Visit Destinations in Switzerland

Zermatt

Begin your journey in Zermatt, a picturesque village at the foot of the Matterhorn, one of the most iconic peaks in the Alps. Zermatt is a car-free zone, adding to its charm and tranquillity. Take the Gornergrat Railway to enjoy panoramic views of the Matterhorn and surrounding mountains, or explore the slopes for skiing, snowboarding, and hiking. Don’t miss the chance to visit the Matterhorn Museum, which offers insights into the history of mountaineering in the region.

Lucerne

Lucerne is one of Switzerland’s most charming cities, known for its well-preserved medieval architecture and stunning lakeside setting. Stroll across the Chapel Bridge, one of the oldest wooden bridges in Europe, and visit the Water Tower, a symbol of the city. Explore the Swiss Transport Museum, which showcases the history of transportation in Switzerland, and take a boat cruise on Lake Lucerne to enjoy the scenic beauty of the surrounding mountains.

Jungfraujoch

Known as the “Top of Europe,” Jungfraujoch is one of the highest train stations in Europe, located at an altitude of 3,454 metres. The journey to Jungfraujoch is an experience in itself, with a scenic train ride through the Swiss countryside, including stops in the charming villages of Lauterbrunnen and Grindelwald. At the summit, you’ll be greeted with breathtaking views of the Aletsch Glacier, the longest glacier in the Alps, as well as the surrounding peaks of the Bernese Alps.

Interlaken

Nestled between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz, Interlaken is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The town is surrounded by the majestic peaks of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau mountains, making it an ideal base for activities such as paragliding, hiking, and skiing. Take a scenic boat cruise on Lake Thun, visit the historic Harder Kulm for panoramic views of the region, and explore the nearby St. Beatus Caves, a natural wonder with underground lakes and waterfalls.

Geneva

Geneva, located on the shores of Lake Geneva, is a cosmopolitan city known for its international organisations, including the United Nations and the Red Cross. Explore the city’s historic Old Town, visit the iconic Jet d’Eau fountain, and take a stroll through the beautiful Jardin Anglais. Geneva is also a great base for exploring nearby attractions, such as the medieval town of Montreux, the Château de Chillon, and the Lavaux Vineyards, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Practical Travel Tips for Switzerland

Best Time to Visit: Switzerland is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on what you want to experience. Summer (June to August) is ideal for hiking, lake activities, and exploring the cities. Winter (December to February) is perfect for skiing and snowboarding. Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer mild weather and beautiful landscapes.

Visa Requirements: Indian citizens travelling to Switzerland require a Schengen visa, as Switzerland is part of the Schengen Area. Apply online well in advance of your travel dates.

Language and Communication: Switzerland has four official languages: German, French, Italian, and Romansh. English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

Currency: The currency in Switzerland is the Swiss Franc (CHF). Credit cards are widely accepted, but it’s advisable to carry some cash for smaller purchases.

Transportation: Switzerland has an excellent public transportation system, including trains, buses, and boats. Consider purchasing a Swiss Travel Pass for unlimited travel within the country.

Cultural Etiquette: Swiss culture values punctuality, cleanliness, and politeness. It’s customary to greet people with a firm handshake and maintain eye contact.

Safety: Switzerland is generally a safe country for travellers, but it’s essential to stay vigilant and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Culinary Delights: Swiss cuisine includes dishes such as fondue, raclette, and rösti. Don’t miss trying Swiss chocolates and cheeses.

Festivals and Events: Switzerland hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year, including the Montreux Jazz Festival in July and the Geneva International Motor Show in March.

Accommodation: Switzerland offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget hostels and charming guesthouses. It’s advisable to book your stay in advance, especially during peak season.
