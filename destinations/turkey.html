Experience the Best of Turkey: Exclusive Tour Packages from India

Turkey, a country where East meets West, offers a captivating blend of ancient history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture. From the iconic city of Istanbul to the otherworldly landscapes of Cappadocia, Turkey is a destination that promises unforgettable experiences. Our Turkey tour packages from India are meticulously crafted to provide you with an immersive journey, ensuring that you experience the very best of this fascinating country.

Explore the Historic Treasures of Istanbul

Your Turkish adventure begins in Istanbul, a city that straddles two continents and is rich in history and culture. Visit the iconic Hagia Sophia, a masterpiece of Byzantine architecture that has served as a church, mosque, and now a museum. Explore the Blue Mosque, known for its stunning blue tiles and grand domes, and take a stroll through the historic Topkapi Palace, the former residence of the Ottoman sultans.

Wander through the bustling Grand Bazaar, one of the oldest and largest covered markets in the world, where you can shop for everything from spices to carpets. Don’t miss the chance to cruise along the Bosphorus, the waterway that separates Europe and Asia, and enjoy panoramic views of Istanbul’s skyline.

Discover the Unique Landscapes of Cappadocia

Cappadocia is one of the most unique and picturesque regions in Turkey, known for its surreal landscapes of fairy chimneys, cave dwellings, and ancient rock formations. Our tour packages include the opportunity to explore Cappadocia’s incredible scenery from both the ground and the air. Take a hot air balloon ride at sunrise to experience the breathtaking beauty of the region from above, and explore the underground cities and rock-cut churches that date back to the early Christian era.

Visit the Goreme Open-Air Museum, a UNESCO World Heritage site, where you can see well-preserved frescoes in rock-hewn churches. Cappadocia is also a great place to experience traditional Turkish culture, with opportunities to stay in cave hotels, enjoy local cuisine, and watch a whirling dervish ceremony.

Relax on Turkey's Stunning Coastline

Turkey’s coastline is dotted with beautiful beaches, charming villages, and ancient ruins. Our tour packages include visits to some of the most popular coastal destinations, including Antalya, known for its turquoise waters and luxury resorts. Explore the ancient city of Aspendos, famous for its well-preserved Roman theatre, and relax on the sandy beaches of Lara and Konyaalti.

For a more laid-back experience, visit the town of Bodrum, known for its whitewashed houses, vibrant nightlife, and historic castle. The nearby Bodrum Peninsula offers stunning views, secluded coves, and crystal-clear waters, perfect for sailing and swimming.

Must-Visit Destinations in Turkey

Istanbul

Begin your journey in Istanbul, the vibrant cultural capital of Turkey. This city is a treasure trove of historic sites, including the iconic Hagia Sophia, the Blue Mosque, and Topkapi Palace. Explore the bustling Grand Bazaar, where you can shop for traditional Turkish goods, and take a cruise along the Bosphorus for stunning views of the city’s skyline. Don’t miss a visit to the Basilica Cistern, an ancient underground water reservoir with impressive architecture.

Cappadocia

Cappadocia is a region of otherworldly landscapes, characterised by its unique rock formations, fairy chimneys, and cave dwellings. Take a hot air balloon ride at sunrise for a breathtaking view of the region, and explore the underground cities and rock-cut churches that date back to the early Christian period. Visit the Goreme Open-Air Museum, a UNESCO World Heritage site, to see well-preserved frescoes and learn about the region’s rich history.

Ephesus

Ephesus is one of the best-preserved ancient cities in the world, offering a glimpse into the grandeur of the Roman Empire. Explore the ruins of the Library of Celsus, the Great Theatre, and the Temple of Artemis, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The nearby House of the Virgin Mary is a significant pilgrimage site, believed to be the final resting place of the Virgin Mary.

Pamukkale

Pamukkale, also known as the “Cotton Castle,” is a natural wonder famous for its white travertine terraces and thermal springs. The ancient city of Hierapolis, located above the terraces, was once a thriving Roman spa town. Explore the ruins of the ancient theatre, the Temple of Apollo, and the ancient baths, and take a dip in Cleopatra’s Pool, a natural thermal pool with submerged ancient columns.

Antalya

Antalya, located on the Turkish Riviera, is a popular destination known for its beautiful beaches, historic old town, and luxury resorts. Explore the ancient ruins of Perge and Aspendos, relax on the beaches of Lara and Konyaalti, and stroll through the charming streets of Kaleici, the historic heart of Antalya.

Practical Travel Tips for Turkey

Best Time to Visit: Turkey is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on the region and the activities you want to enjoy. Spring (April to June) and autumn (September to November) are ideal for exploring cities and historical sites. Summer (June to August) is perfect for beach vacations, while winter (December to February) is the best time to visit Cappadocia for hot air ballooning and skiing.

Visa Requirements: Indian citizens travelling to Turkey require a visa. The most common visa for tourism is the e-Visa, which allows you to stay in Turkey for up to 30 days. Apply online well in advance of your travel dates.

Language and Communication: Turkish is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learning a few Turkish phrases can enhance your experience.

Currency: The currency in Turkey is the Turkish Lira (TRY). Credit and debit cards are widely accepted in cities, but it’s advisable to carry cash for small purchases, especially in rural areas.

Transportation: Turkey has a well-developed transportation network, including buses, flights, and trains. Domestic flights are a convenient way to travel between major cities, while buses connect most towns and regions.

Cultural Etiquette: Turkish people are known for their hospitality. It’s customary to greet people with a smile and maintain polite conversation. Dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques and religious sites.

Safety: Turkey is generally safe for travellers, but as with any destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

Culinary Delights: Turkish cuisine is diverse and flavorful. Don’t miss trying traditional dishes like kebabs, baklava, and Turkish tea. Street food, such as simit and börek, is also popular and delicious.

Festivals and Events: Turkey hosts numerous festivals, including the Istanbul Film Festival, Cappadocia Hot Air Balloon Festival, and International Antalya Sand Sculpture Festival.

Accommodation: Turkey offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts to boutique hotels and budget hostels. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons.
